Ali Zaoua

Directed by: Nabil Ayouch

Media Details

"Ali Zaoua: Prince of the Streets" is a Moroccan drama film directed by Nabil Ayouch, which was released on May 18, 2000. The movie is in Moroccan Arabic and has a runtime of 99 minutes.

The film revolves around the story of four young boys who live on the streets of Casablanca, Morocco. They are Ali, Kouka, Omar, and Boubker. The boys dream of a better life, away from the harsh realities of the streets. They spend their days hustling for money, and their nights dreaming of escape.

One day, Ali, who is the leader of the group, is killed in a fight with a rival gang. The boys decide to give Ali a proper burial and fulfill his dream of sailing away to a better life. They embark on a dangerous journey to steal a boat, encountering various obstacles along the way.

The film explores themes such as poverty, violence, and the resilience of the human spirit. It also highlights the struggles of children who grow up on the streets, and their desire to break free from their harsh reality.

"Ali Zaoua: Prince of the Streets" received critical acclaim and won several awards, including the Golden Spike at the Valladolid International Film Festival in Spain. The movie is a powerful and emotional portrayal of the lives of street children, and it has been praised for its realism and authenticity.
