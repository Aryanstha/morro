Mimosas

Directed by: Oliver Laxe

Media Details

"Mimosas" is a drama film directed by Oliver Laxe, which premiered at the Cannes Film Festival in 2016. The movie was released on May 19, 2016, and has a runtime of 96 minutes. The language used in the film is a mixture of Arabic and Spanish.

The film tells the story of a group of travelers who journey through the Atlas Mountains in Morocco, trying to escort a dying sheikh to his final resting place. Ahmed, a young man who joins the group, is tasked with leading the sheikh's caravan through the rugged terrain. Along the way, they encounter various obstacles, including bandits, treacherous landscapes, and harsh weather conditions.

The film explores themes such as faith, destiny, and the human condition. It portrays the spiritual and physical journey of the characters as they grapple with their own beliefs and the challenges they face on their journey.

"Mimosas" received critical acclaim and won the Nespresso Grand Prize at the Cannes Film Festival in 2016. The movie is a visually stunning and thought-provoking portrayal of the human experience and the search for meaning in life.
