Mausoleum of Sultan Mohammed

Design Details

The Mausoleum of Sultan Mohammed V is a mausoleum located in the Moroccan capital city of Rabat. It was established in 1961 by King Hassan II of Morocco in honor of his father, Sultan Mohammed V, who was the King of Morocco from 1957 until his death in 1961. The mausoleum also houses the tombs of Sultan Mohammed V's two sons, King Hassan II and Prince Abdallah.

The Mausoleum of Sultan Mohammed V is a stunning example of modern Moroccan architecture. The building covers an area of approximately 25,000 square meters and features a green tiled roof, ornate bronze doors, and intricate stucco work. The interior of the mausoleum is equally impressive, with marble floors, walls covered in Arabic calligraphy, and a large central chamber that houses the tombs of the three royals.

The mausoleum is located in the heart of Rabat, in a large square that is surrounded by other important landmarks, including the Hassan Tower and the Royal Palace. The square is a popular destination for both tourists and locals, and it is a symbol of the rich history and culture of Morocco.

The Mausoleum of Sultan Mohammed V is considered to be one of the most important landmarks in Morocco and is a testament to the country's rich cultural heritage. It is a must-see destination for anyone visiting Rabat, and it is a reminder of the important role that the royal family has played in shaping the history and identity of Morocco.
