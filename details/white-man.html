The White Man

By Lalla Essaydi

Art Details

Lalla Essaydi's artwork "The White Man" is a photograph that depicts a group of women dressed in traditional Moroccan clothing, lying down and looking directly at the viewer. The women are surrounded by various objects, such as books, pottery, and textiles, and the background is covered in Arabic calligraphy.

The title of the artwork is a direct challenge to the notion of Western dominance and the idea that the Western perspective is the only valid one. By placing the women in a position of power and agency, Essaydi subverts the traditional Western portrayal of Middle Eastern women as passive and oppressed.

The calligraphy in the background also adds to the subversion, as it is often associated with Islamic art and culture, which is often viewed as foreign and exotic in the West.

Overall, "The White Man explain this" challenges the viewer to question their own assumptions and biases about different cultures, and to consider the complex and multifaceted nature of identity and representation.
