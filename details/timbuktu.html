Timbuktu

Directed by: Abderragmane Sissako

Media Details

"Timbuktu" is a drama film directed by Abderrahmane Sissako, which premiered at the Cannes Film Festival in 2014. The film was released on December 10, 2014, in France, and has a runtime of 97 minutes. The language used in the movie is a mixture of French, Arabic, and Bambara.

The film is set in Timbuktu, a city in Mali, during the occupation by Islamist militants in 2012. It depicts the lives of the local people who are subjected to the harsh and oppressive rules of the jihadists. The film follows the story of a cattle herder named Kidane, who lives outside the city with his wife and daughter. His peaceful life is disrupted when he gets into a conflict with a fisherman who kills one of his cows. This confrontation has disastrous consequences for Kidane and his family.

The film deals with themes such as religious extremism, oppression, and the effects of war on civilians. It portrays the daily struggles of the people living under the rule of the jihadists and their efforts to maintain their dignity and humanity.
