Media Details

Bahia Palace is a historical palace located in the Mellah district of Marrakech, Morocco. The palace was established in the late 19th century, between 1866 and 1867, by Ahmed Ben Moussa, who was the Grand Vizier of the sultan at that time. The palace was built on a vast area of 20,000 square meters, making it one of the largest palaces in Morocco.

The palace was designed to be a representation of the Islamic and Moroccan architectural styles of that time. It features intricate stucco work, wooden carvings, and Zellige tilework, which is a type of Moroccan mosaic tilework. The palace is adorned with beautiful gardens, fountains, and courtyards, which add to its grandeur.

The palace was named Bahia, which means "brilliance," as it was meant to be a symbol of the brilliance of Moroccan architecture and craftsmanship. The palace was also designed to be a residence for the wives and concubines of the Grand Vizier, and it was later used as a residence by other high-ranking officials.

Today, the Bahia Palace is open to the public, and visitors can explore its many rooms, courtyards, and gardens. The palace is a popular tourist attraction in Marrakech and is considered to be one of the most beautiful palaces in Morocco.
